How to make a Safeguarding Complaint for a child



Safeguarding is about keeping people safe from abuse and harm.

As a member of the public you should report to Tusla if you have a ‘reasonable concern’ about the welfare of a child.

There are lots of Guidelines about reporting a safeguarding concern about children. These guidelines are called Children First.

Some adults who work with Children and see abuse must report to Tusla by law

If you are worried about a child who may be suffering abuse you should contact TUSLA. There is a contact in each county at this link

TUSLA Contact

You will have to give the child’s name and address, who is abusing the child and other information

An Garda Síochána (Police)

If your complaint involves a possible crime contact your local Garda Station
